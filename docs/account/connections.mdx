Connect your accounts to log in and participate in matches.

## Login Providers

These accounts let you sign in to TeamBattles:

- Discord
- Twitch
- GitHub
- Battle.net

### Managing Login Providers

**Link a new provider:**

1. Go to **Settings > Connections**
2. Click **Connect** next to the provider
3. Authorize in the popup

**Unlink a provider:**

1. Go to **Settings > Connections**
2. Click **Disconnect** next to the provider

<Warning>
	You must keep at least one login provider connected. Unlink your last provider by connecting a
	different one first.
</Warning>

## Connection Visibility

Control whether others can see your connected platforms on your profile. This setting is separate from your profile visibility.

For streaming platforms (Twitch, YouTube, Kick), the **Show on Profile** toggle also gates the [livestream indicator](/account/livestream) - if it is off, your live badge will not appear, even when you are actually live.

## Verified vs. self-reported connections

Each connection shows a shield icon that tells you whether ownership has been proven:

| Shield | Meaning |
| ------ | ------- |
| Green ShieldCheck | **Verified** - You completed the platform's OAuth authorization flow, so TeamBattles holds a real token from the platform that proves ownership. |
| Yellow ShieldAlert | **Self-reported** - You typed in a username manually. There is no proof you own the account. |

Under the hood, every connection carries an `isSelfReported` flag. Connections written by the OAuth connect callback are verified; connections added via the manual "type a username" flow are flagged as self-reported and rendered with the unverified shield wherever they appear on public profiles.

<Tip>
	If you have a self-reported connection that you can actually authorize, disconnect it and reconnect
	through the platform's OAuth flow to get the verified shield.
</Tip>

## Account login conflicts

If you try to log in with an OAuth account whose verified email matches an **existing** TeamBattles account, the login does **not** silently merge into that account. Instead, the server records a short-lived conflict and refuses to authenticate the attempted session.

**How it works:**

1. The OAuth callback detects that the verified email already belongs to another user.
2. A row is written to the `authConflicts` table with a 5-minute expiry.
3. While the conflict exists, any authenticated request for the affected user is rejected, so the attempted login cannot act on the existing account.
4. A cleanup job runs every 5 minutes to drop expired conflict rows.

**When this fires:**

- Conflicts only apply to OAuth providers that return a **verified** email - Discord, Twitch, GitHub, and Google.
- Battle.net never collides on email because it does not return one.

**What to do:**

1. Sign out of the conflicting OAuth provider.
2. Log in with the existing account using its original login provider.
3. Open **Settings > Connections** and link the new provider from there.

This way the new provider becomes an additional login method for your existing account instead of trying to create a separate account on the same email.

<Warning>
	If you do not control the existing account that owns the email, do not try to "claim" it by
	repeatedly logging in. Contact support instead.
</Warning>

## Troubleshooting

**"I can't link my Activision account"**

- Ensure you're logged into the correct Activision account
- Try logging out of Activision and back in
- Clear your browser cache

**"I can't unlink my Discord"**

- You must keep at least one login provider
- Connect a different provider first, then unlink
