---
title: "Motion paths"
description: "Author smooth motion between stages with drag-recorded, manual, or automatic paths."
---

When a token moves between stages, you can shape exactly how it gets from A to B. Motion paths render in the editor as curves on the ghost layer and play through smoothly during playback or GIF export.

## Three modes

### Drag-recorded (default)

Drag a token on a new stage and the cursor trace becomes its motion path automatically. The path follows your hand as you drag.

### Manual

Switch to **Manual** in the property panel's **Motion path** section. The path becomes editable on canvas:

- **Double-click the path** to insert a new control point at that location.
- **Drag a control point** to bend the curve.
- **Right-click a control point** to remove it.

You can have up to 32 control points per path.

### Automatic

Switch to **Automatic** to render a perpendicular-offset curve. The **Curvature** slider (0 to 1) controls how far the path bows from a straight line.

## How paths follow the endpoints

Paths are stored relative to the token's stage-K-1 and stage-K positions. If you later reposition either endpoint, the path's shape stretches with it, so you don't have to re-author when you move the token.

## Resetting

Choose **Linear** in the property panel to clear the path and revert to a straight A to B move.

## How the path affects token facing

When you author or edit a motion path on a **player token**, the editor sets the token's rotation on the source stage (where the path starts) to match the path's **initial heading**, and on the destination stage (where the path ends) to match the path's **terminal heading**. This keeps the token's silhouette pointing along the direction it is actually moving, instead of staying frozen in whatever rotation it had before you authored the path.

This applies whether you draw the path by dragging (the default), by editing control points manually, or by toggling the Automatic curvature - any motion-path edit triggers the rewrite.

<Note>
	The rotation is computed from the path geometry with a 90-degree silhouette
	correction (player silhouettes are drawn nose-up, so the geometric tangent angle
	needs +90 to align the player's nose with the direction of motion).
</Note>

<Note>
	This auto-rotation applies to live-player tokens only. Equipment, objectives,
	and other non-player tokens keep whatever rotation you authored on them, even
	after you draw a motion path between stages.
</Note>

### Trade-off in this version

For player tokens, authoring a new path **overwrites** any rotation you had previously set by hand on the source or destination row. If you want to keep a manual rotation on a player token, set it AFTER you author the path - the path won't re-author it on its own, and your manual rotation will stick until the next time you re-drag (or otherwise edit) the same path.

A future version may add an "auto-rotate from path" toggle so manual rotations can coexist with path-driven rotations. For now, manual-then-path is the supported workflow for player tokens.
