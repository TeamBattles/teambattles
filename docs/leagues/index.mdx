Leagues bring structure to competitive play. They let organizers define rules, manage seasons, track standings, and build communities around their favorite games.

## What Leagues Offer

- **Structured competition** - Defined rules, seasons, and point systems
- **Standings and leaderboards** - Track team performance with a configurable points engine
- **Rule enforcement** - Match settings are validated automatically at match creation and acceptance
- **Season management** - Run multiple seasons with separate stats and standings
- **Team management** - Approve applications, issue penalties, and manage rosters
- **Staff roles** - Delegate responsibilities across your admin team
- **Support tickets** - Handle disputes and questions from league participants

## How Leagues Work

1. A user applies to create a league through TeamBattles
2. Once approved, the league owner configures games, rules, and point settings
3. Teams apply to join the league and are reviewed by league staff
4. A season is created and activated to begin tracking stats
5. Teams compete in matches - points are awarded and standings update in real time
6. When a season ends, standings are finalized and a new season can begin

## League Roles

| Role        | Description                                                                 |
| ----------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Owner**   | Full control over the league. Can transfer ownership and shut down the league. |
| **Admin**   | Manages teams, staff, seasons, rules, and settings. Cannot transfer ownership. |
| **Manager** | Approves or denies team applications. Issues penalties and manages rosters.  |
| **Member**  | Basic staff access. Can view admin dashboard and activity logs.              |

<Note>League roles are for league staff - not for teams competing in the league. Teams join through the application process.</Note>
